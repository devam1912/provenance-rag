# Transfer Credit Policy

This policy governs the evaluation and acceptance of academic credits completed at external institutions for transfer toward undergraduate degrees.

## 1. Acceptable Institutions
The University accepts transfer credits from institutions accredited by recognized regional accrediting bodies.
*   Credits from unaccredited institutions are subject to review by the Dean of the relevant college and require a syllabus review.
*   International credentials must be evaluated by a NACES-approved credential evaluation service.

## 2. Maximum Transfer Credits
There are strict caps on the number of transfer credits that can be applied to a degree program:
*   Two-Year Colleges: A maximum of 60 credit hours can be transferred from two-year or community colleges.
*   Four-Year Colleges: A maximum of 90 credit hours can be transferred from four-year institutions.
*   Total Combined Cap: In no case can a student transfer more than 90 credit hours in total from all external sources combined (including AP/IB exam credits).

## 3. Minimum Grade Requirements
To transfer, courses must meet minimum grade requirements:
*   Courses completed with a grade of C (2.00 on a 4.00 scale) or higher are eligible for transfer.
*   Courses with grades of C- (1.67) or below are not eligible for transfer.
*   Grades earned in transfer courses do not calculate into the student's cumulative GPA at the University; only the credit hours transfer.
*   Pass/Fail (or Satisfactory/Unsatisfactory) courses may transfer only if the transcript contains official documentation indicating that a Pass grade is equivalent to a C or higher.

## 4. Evaluation and Expiration
Transfer credits are evaluated by the Office of Admissions.
*   Science and technology courses completed more than 10 years prior to admission are subject to review and may not be accepted for major requirement credit.
*   To receive credit for a specific University course, the transfer course content must be at least 70% equivalent to the University's course syllabus.
