# Graduation Requirements Policy

This policy outlines the credit, residency, and grade requirements that undergraduate students must meet to qualify for graduation and receive a bachelor's degree.

## 1. Credit Requirements
All undergraduate bachelor's degree programs require the successful completion of a minimum of 120 credit hours.
*   Of the 120 minimum credit hours, at least 45 credit hours must be at the upper-division level (courses numbered 3000 or above).
*   Developmental or remedial courses (e.g., courses numbered below 1000) do not count toward the 120 credit hour graduation requirement.
*   A maximum of 4 credit hours of physical education activity courses can count toward graduation.

## 2. Grade Point Average (GPA) Requirements
To qualify for graduation, students must meet two distinct GPA thresholds:
*   Cumulative GPA: Students must achieve a minimum cumulative GPA of 2.00 in all course work completed at the University.
*   Major GPA: Students must achieve a minimum GPA of 2.50 in all courses designated as part of their major requirements.
*   All courses required for the major must be completed with a grade of C- or higher. Courses with grades of D or F do not fulfill major requirements.

## 3. Residency Requirements
Residency requirements specify the minimum number of credits that must be taken directly at our University.
*   Students must complete a minimum of 30 credit hours in residence at the University.
*   A student's final 30 credit hours immediately preceding graduation must be completed in residence at the University.
*   Credits earned via credit-by-exam (e.g., AP, IB, CLEP) do not count as residency credits.

## 4. Graduation Application
Students must formally apply for graduation through the Registrar's Office.
*   The application must be submitted by the end of the second week of the semester in which the student intends to graduate.
*   Late applications are subject to a $50 late fee.
