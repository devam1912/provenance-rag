# Academic Standing Policy

This policy governs student academic performance thresholds, academic warning, academic probation, suspension, and Dean's List eligibility at the University.

## 1. Good Academic Standing
To remain in good academic standing, all undergraduate students must maintain a cumulative Grade Point Average (GPA) of 2.00 or higher. Satisfactory progress is evaluated at the end of the Fall and Spring semesters.

## 2. Academic Warning
Students whose term GPA falls below 2.00 in any single semester, but whose cumulative GPA remains above 2.00, will be placed on Academic Warning.
*   An academic warning is a notice to the student that their academic progress has slipped.
*   Academic warnings do not appear on official student transcripts.
*   Students on academic warning are encouraged to meet with their academic advisor to discuss success strategies.

## 3. Academic Probation
Students whose cumulative GPA falls below 2.00 will be placed on Academic Probation.
*   Students on Academic Probation are limited to a maximum course load of 13 credit hours per semester.
*   Probation status is recorded on the student's official transcript.
*   To return to Good Academic Standing, a student on Academic Probation must raise their cumulative GPA to 2.00 or higher by the end of the subsequent semester of enrollment.
*   Failure to raise the cumulative GPA to 2.00 or higher within one semester on Academic Probation will lead to Academic Suspension.

## 4. Academic Suspension
A student on Academic Probation who fails to raise their cumulative GPA to 2.00 or higher by the end of their probation semester will be suspended from the University.
*   First Suspension: The student is suspended for a minimum of one calendar semester (excluding Summer terms) and must apply for reinstatement.
*   Second Suspension: If a reinstated student falls below good academic standing again and is suspended a second time, the suspension is for a minimum of two calendar years.
*   Reinstatement: Reinstatement is not automatic and requires an appeal to the Academic Review Committee.

## 5. Dean's List Honors
At the end of each Fall and Spring semester, students who have achieved exceptional academic records are recognized on the Dean's List.
*   To be eligible, a student must complete at least 12 graded credit hours (excluding Pass/Fail courses) during the semester.
*   The student's term GPA for that semester must be 3.50 or higher.
*   Students with a grade of F, I (Incomplete), or W (Withdrawal) in the current semester are ineligible for Dean's List honors.
